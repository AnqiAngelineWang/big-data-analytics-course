Objectives

Big Data analytics has been transforming industry and science in various domains for the past few years, making possible the processing of Terabytes of data on a daily basis. This was enabled by the joint evolution of programming models and computing infrastructures.

This course introduces the concepts and some of the main algorithms used for Big Data analytics. It presents the principles of the Hadoop ecosystem and it details the main algorithms for the analysis of large datasets, related to similarity search, mining of frequent itemsets, graph analysis, clustering, stream mining, recommendation systems and advertising. It concludes with a brief review of infrastructures to deploy Hadoop.

By the end of this course, students will be able to write and deploy efficient parallel algorithms to analyze Big Data sources for various applications.

Course Evaluation

Reading quizzes (5%): there will be weekly reading assignments for which you will be asked to read a few sections from the recommended books. Reading assignments will be announced at least one week in advance. Short quizzes (3 to 5 questions) will take place every week during the lectures (see schedule table) to evaluate your understanding of the material. Among the 10 quizzes, your will be entitled to 3 jokers, i.e., I will remove your worst 3 reading marks (e.g. low mark or no mark because of missed class). There will be no substitution for a missed quiz. Quizzes will be closed-book.

Lab assignments (15%): you will be required to develop data analysis programs. There will be a total of two assignments. You must work on these assignments individually or in a team of two. The lab assignments are due for the Monday morning 8am following the lab session (see exact dates on the schedule table). Deadlines are firm, no extension will be granted. Assignments must be submitted through Moodle.

Mid-term exam (40%): the exam is a closed-book exam and will be conducted on the date indicated on the schedule table, during the lecture. In general, you will need to bring your own ENCS calculator. There will be no substitution for a missed exam. Passing the mid-term exam is necessary for passing the course.

Project (40%): In the project you will have to implement an application involving Big Data Analytics. You can work on the project individually or in a team of two. The project will have the following milestones. Deadlines are indicated on the schedule. No deadline extension will be granted.

  1. The project proposal (10%) will be a document of 3 pages or less with the following structure:
    • Abstract: a few sentences summarizing the document.
    • I. Introduction: context, presentation of the problem to solve, related work.
    • II. Materials and Methods: the dataset(s) and algorithms that will be used.
    The project proposal will be evaluated based on its clarity, originality and technical quality.
  2. The project report (20%) will be a document of 6 pages or less with the following structure:
    • Abstract: as in the project proposal.
    • I. Introduction: as in the proposal.
    • II. Materials and Methods: as in the proposal.
    • III. Results: a description of the output of the algorithm(s) implemented, possibly with performance evaluations.
    • IV. Discussion: a discussion of the relevance of the solution, of its limitations and of possible future work.
    The project report will be evaluated based on its clarity, originality and technical quality.
  3. The project presentation (10%) will be a 10-minute presentation of the project.
Project proposals and reports will be submitted through Moodle.

Grading Scheme: A passing mark on each of the 4 deliverables (reading quizzes, lab assignments, mid-term exam and project) is required to get a passing grade for the course. There is no standard relationship between percentages and letter grades assigned. The grading of the course will be done based on the relative percentages assigned to the assignments and the exams. There is no definite rule for translation of number grades to letter grades.
