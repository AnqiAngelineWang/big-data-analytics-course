    Available online.

Assignments

Readings (5%): there will be weekly reading assignments for which you will be asked to read a few sections from the recommended books. Reading assignments will be announced at least one week in advance. Short weekly quizzes (3 to 5 questions) will take place during the lectures (see overview table) to evaluate your understanding of the material. Among the 10 quizzes, your will be entitled to 3 jokers, i.e., I will remove your worst 3 reading marks (e.g. low mark or no mark because of missed class).

Lab assignments (15%): you will be required to develop data analysis programs. There will be a total of two assignments. You must work on these assignments individually or in a team of two. The lab assignments are due for the Monday morning 8am following the lab session (see exact dates on the overview table).

Mid-term exam (40%): the exam is a closed-book exam and will be conducted on the date indicated on the overview table, during the lecture. In general, you will need to bring your own ENCS calculator. There will be no substitution for a missed exam. Passing the mid-term exam is necessary for passing the course.

Project (40%): In the project you will have to implement an application involving Big Data Analytics. You can work on the project individually or in a team of two. The project will be submitted as follows.

  1. The project proposal (10%) will be a document of 3 pages or less with the following structure:
    • Abstract: a few sentences summarizing the document.
    • I. Introduction: context, presentation of the problem to solve, related work.
    • II. Materials and Methods: the dataset(s) and algorithms that will be used.
  2. The project report (20%) will be a document of 6 pages or less with the following structure:
    • Abstract: as in the project proposal.
    • I. Introduction: as in the proposal.
    • II. Materials and Methods: as in the proposal.
    • III. Results: a description of the output of the algorithm(s) implemented, possibly with performance evaluations.
    • IV. Discussion: a discussion of the relevance of the solution, of its limitations and of possible future work.
  3. The project presentation (10%) will be a 10-minute presentation of the project.
